How nonprofits use MapVest

Real examples of how a shared map can make a difference

🚨

Landmine reporting

Map contaminated areas and unexploded ordnance, share data with field teams and plan demining operations safely.

🍲

Food collection centers

Coordinate food collection and distribution, map donors, kitchens and beneficiaries, optimize delivery routes.

👵

Care for elderly and vulnerable people

Locate people who need care or hot meals, organize home visits and monitor coverage across the territory.

🐾

Endangered species

Map sightings of at-risk species, coordinate field monitoring and share data with the scientific community.

🆘

Rescue operations

Coordinate volunteers in emergencies, map shelters and aid centers, plan resources on the ground in real time.

💧

Access to clean water

Map wells, fountains and water sources, monitor status and maintenance, identify areas with water scarcity.

🏥

Mobile clinics and health outposts

Locate health facilities, organize medical campaigns, coordinate mobile teams in the most remote areas.

📚

Schools and education projects

Map supported institutions, coordinate education volunteers and monitor training projects across the territory.

Reforestation and conservation

Geolocate intervention areas, log plantings and biodiversity surveys, track project progress over time.

Housing distress mapping

Identify homes that need intervention, organize volunteer crews and plan support activities on the ground.

🐕

Animal welfare

Map cat colonies, strays and reports of mistreatment, coordinate sterilizations and veterinary interventions.

♻️

Environmental initiatives

Organize cleanup days, map polluted spots and collection points, coordinate volunteers across the territory.
